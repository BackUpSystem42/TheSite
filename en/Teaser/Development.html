Juvenile

Teaser 8 is technically one of oldest stories that I made, but I did not start working on it until I was in college. It was story that I made when I was a kid. I did not think about them until I was helping my parents donate some old things and toys. Then I remembered them.

When I played with my cousin, he always wanted to be the hero. I played as the villain. These three are the generals of some villain organization. Think of them as early 2000s Power Rangers Villains. (My cousin’s favorite show/franchise). They do very common villain things. Building Death Ray, Stealing Jewels, Kidnapping Damsel in Distress, etc.

One of the adventures ended a little differently. The leader of organization, it did not have a name, ended up as a hand in a jar. The leader was a large shadow monster. The three generals were trying to revive the leader, which is why the hand in is a large jar. Then I said both to myself and in character, “I do not know how to fix it.” I felt hopeless and made these characters feel hopeless as well. That was the last time I played as these three characters.

When I remember them; I asked, “What are they doing now?” Did they revive their leader? Did they want to revive the leader? Did they disband? How did they form in the first place? What opportunity made them this way?

The story starts where I left off.

This is another childhood character, but only when I am by myself. The character in the middle is basically a monster tamer. This robot is not the strongest. It has to outthink its opponent. It never defeat/kill/destroy its enemies. As a kid, it was the power of friendship. Now, it saw so many machines and robots rust away. It cannot handle seeing more meet the same fate. Blind faith or hopefulness. It rather tries to teach them to do better than end them.

It lives in a quarantine area. It is the city and some of the neighboring areas. It was evacuated not that long ago, no more than 20 years. This is one of many places that had a robot uprising. The robot is just here and tries to live its life. It is indifferent from both sides, the human and machine. It just wants the violence to end.

I had a dream that stood with me. I am not sure when I had it. It was about a man walking in an old castle. It is in ruins. How this person moves around it, it seems he is familiar with the place. There is nothing left. How he looks at the air, it seems that there should be a wall, a painting, something. A longing look. A bit sad. He is trying to rebuild on brick of time.

The castle grows in odd ways. A tower was built; there were no stairs. The second floor was complete but cannot be reached. The castle is more of a living thing than object.





Teens

This goes without saying, but being teenager is an awkward time. For me, I did not fully understand others at my own age. For example, I was doing classwork and I overheard my classmates talking. They were talking about another fight in the cafeteria. I was disgusted. Not the fact they were talking about, but they were enjoying that people got hurt. I thought I should say something. Then another thought appeared. “Should I really take away their happiness?” They did not cause the fight. Practically no one is really happy being here. Should I spoil it. But should they really be thinking that way. It is not my life they are living, so should I say something?

I have gone back and forth. I always ended at a moral stalemate. But I was not happy that I could not make a decision in those situations. So, I wanted to learn more about why I cannot choose. I heard writing down your thoughts help. But I know I would treat it like a report. Just look at the face value. Then, I chose to write a story. All I did was study math and science. I did not have a creative outlet. There is no harm in trying. It is at least good practice on writing, which I was not the best at.

I wrote a story called Attributes. It was a story about a person documenting these creatures which he called “Attributes”. They are basically the angel and devil on your shoulder. All they do is speak. They cannot do any physical harm. They are the voices in your head. Everyone has at least one of each type. It was shouting match on which one would be heard. For example, there is an Attribute that talks about being afraid, creating fear, and one helping end fear for others.

As I was writing the Attribute story, I noticed a problem. It is how people will interpret the story. How they consume and interact with the content. Everyone has good and bad Attributes. It is a fight for balance. A constant negotiation with yourself. That is the goal of the story. I can see someone saying that “I only have the good ones”. That is not the point. Many people say being afraid is a bad thing. But courage will never be born if fear did not exist first. There was another problem that is too many characters to manage. Like 100+.

I did not have a solution for a long while. Not until I was in college. So, this story about the Attributes is permanently beached. I have no plans on making this story in the original form.

So, I have to redo the whole story. I need to make these magical creatures that cannot be removed or excluded. Everyone has one of each voice, no exception. They have to represent something that if removed, you will no longer behave like a person. That they have to be neutral. As true neutral as possible. They do not promote nor end hate. They do not inspire nor stop joy. The negativity that they might represent is our perception. The same creature will be an angel for one person and a devil for another.

The three that you see in the teaser image are the oldest and the most influential. They are not called Attribute anymore. I want to separate it from its roots.

They are there to watch. They are curious about how your life, your story, will end. They speak to give advice. Or might be a trick to make you worse. It is not clear. No matter how your story ends, they will remember you. They will never forget your story. Another book to their ever-growing library.

Without going into too much detail, each one does not represent a single concept. Like the fear example from before. Now they are the vessel or tool for emotion or behavior. One of them represents memory. That specific being wants you to remember everything. All the good and bad days. There is no negotiation on forgetting something.

You will remember. Every. Detail. Crystal. Clear.

I tried to write in a different direction with the original Attribute story. The same type of magical creature, but different rules of magic. Another universe. I had a question about them. What was first? What characteristic, behavior, virtue was the first that sparked everything. I tried creating a character that has everything. This overpower and all-knowing being. The source of everything.

I did not like it. I think it was the fact that if one has everything, then everyone else is useless. Why bother creating or have people around if one person can do everything.

This being is a shapeshifter. It can take any form that it needs. It also has the skill to make its tools. It has a tool or equipment for almost every occasion. The shapeshifting ability and weapon expertise are completely different behavior. But I have the same question for both. What am I really good at?

So, I spilt that entity into two characters, shown in the picture. The taller one is the shapeshifter, and the smaller one is the tool maker. I removed the original core concept of the Attribute story. The idea of creatures influencing us, but without physical interaction. These characters are old and forgotten gods. One of firsts. They were there when it all started.

I have new questions for them. If you see everything, know everything, is there anything left in the world? Why are you there? Do you even have a desire? Do you feel more comfortable with yourself if someone else is experiencing the same thing?

I wanted to try again on writing the shapeshifting toolmaker character. One character with both abilities.

Why did I not like it?

That character was too overpowerful, and I cannot find any motivation for them. No conflict that they can resolve easily. Basically, a Mary Sue.

Are its abilities the reason why the character has no motivation?

No, there are a lot of shapeshifters in other media like Clay Face from Batman, Rex Salazar from Generator Rex or the Gems from Steven Universe. Often, they shapeshift a part of themselves into a weapon. Their abilities are ridiculous, but they are not the most powerful or most dangerous characters in their respective stories. Their flaws are more internal than external.

Is this character the only one that exists?

I want them to do be. But why is there just one? It is the only that exists for some reason. They are the master creators of everything. Last of their kind. Only one that was made.

Made. Created. Instead of a shapeshifting toolmaker. It should be a shapeshifting tool. A shifting weapon. There are other media that mention a powerful special weapon, and the twist ended up as a person or a child. Something in that nature.

The character is the teaser image is a living weapon. It takes any form to complete its task. It wants to fulfill its purpose. The reason why it was created. But feel that its master is preventing that. It can do more than what it is currently allowed to do. It is thinking why and spiraling into a whirlwind of thoughts. Then it asked, “why am I being restricted?”

Why was I made in this way? I can do so much more. My master can easily make me with less power to fit my purpose. What is my master gain for doing this to me?

When I was designing the smaller character in the Teaser 15 image, this was an alternative design. The character is a tool maker and has large amounts of equipment at its disposal. In Teaser 15, the guy just infinitely pulls weapons and equipment from under its cloak.

I thought since its equipment is a core characteristic; why not make the character a walking suit of armor. When it falls into pieces it will reassemble itself. I liked the idea, but it did fit the direction of the story.

So, made a story just of it. Instead of a main role, it is a side character. More of a force, than a person. An object that needs guide. The armor is like a wild animal.

The smaller character in Teaser 15 image has a lot of weapons with strange powers.

I was doing research for inspiration with taboo or evil power in other media. Like mind controlling or power stealing.

I made this crown and sword in Teaser 12. Both have abilities that are involved with control. The sword can puppeteer the person that it cuts. With the crown, you can take the face of all previous wearers. It reminds me of another story, in a superficial way.

If those characters have these weapons and powers, what will they do? The people in the background are more cursed to use these items. They did not find these items. They are permanently connected to them. Do they even want that power? If they have a choice to be more normal, would they take it?





College

I was taking a game design class. Both for fun and to meet an art course requirement. One of the assignments was to make a board game based on an image of a long-forgotten board game.

I enjoy the assignment. I continued thinking about different board game ideas long after the assignment. I made one for myself. It is a game that is basically chess with cards.

The world is built around collecting cards and finding elements to make game pieces. The big monster is a golem. The gameplay with the board controls the golem. The golem is a friend, a protector. You and your golem go into the unknown to find adventure. Your golem is scary looking, but it is a big sweetheart. Like a big puppy.

This one came around by accident. I was in my dorm and had a dream. When I woke up, I spent most of the morning drawing this. (I know it is not a good drawing). The entire dream was in an aerial preserve. I saw this island filled with large statues. There are no other structures. No houses. No towns. No farms. Just these statues. Some of the statues were buried, in pieces, or standing alone. There was this large pale tree. It was hollowed out with carvings of some history. When I left the tree, all the statues started to move. They unearth themselves. They put themselves back together. They walk toward me.

The character in the teaser image is one of the must powers of these statues. All it has to do is stand in one place. It is a statue made of metal. Iron, copper, nickel, gold, lead. It can stand in a waterway, and everything around will die. It knows what it is capable of. It is fully aware.

I have been working on and off this project throughout college. I know it is over complicated and too ambitious project. At the time, I did not think of selling it. A game just for me. Something for fun. And something to practice coding. This project is something I want to see it to the end. So, it will be a later project to work on.

When I was designing different statues for Teaser 9’s world, I was making those characters too complex. The island of statues was interesting to me. The interest is that there is a mystery, but the active party, the statues, cannot say it. The statues have behaviors, but only a few have a personality. The statues are objects. There is no reasoning with them. I was making a person and not a callus object. Only motivation of those statues have is you should not be on their island.

However, I like the idea of a society or a world of creatures that behavior like those statues. Not giants, but entities that can put them back together when someone tears them apart. I was making clans/factions, and they started to look familiar to me. It reminded me of my old Attribute story. As I was making the characters for this project, I was working on Teaser 13 story as well. So, there might be some overlapping in design.

The four in the image are members of a council. They are on a metaphorical island. A place that isolated from rest of the world. They did not want to be there but ended up there. They found a way out of that miserable place, but they could not leave. There are so many more stuck there. They made this place theirs. They made the council and became members. They do not care what you are, but who you want to be

The thing I like about RPG is the discovery. Not the exploration. It is assumed that exploration is discovery. But you can discover something new without going somewhere. Going to a new place is not exciting for me. Learning about a place is. The collecting aspect is my favorite part. Fulling out an encyclopedia or a bestiary.

So, this game is built around archiving and examining the monster not just killing monsters. What are their weaknesses? What resources can I get from them? Do they have a natural enemy?

It is a twist on Teaser 14. It is monster hunting game. Instead of using some element system to boost attacks, the elements are just status effects.

Each enemy can apply a status effect. You can only lose the effect either waiting it out or intentionally receiving another status that neutralizes it. For example, if you have freeze, you can cancel it out with burn.

If you are wondering why this character is more fleshed out than Teaser 14. Teaser 14 has two silhouettes, and this one has a fully design character. It is not. So why is the cop there? Just because. This teaser is more of a fun or joke game. Something that is not meant to be taken seriously. The person is cop because it was based on that Mall Cop FNaF meme. I found it a little funny. As I think more about it, a mall is a perfect environment to have elemental monsters. The ice monster is near that frozen yogurt shop, the fire monster hangs out by the food court, the rock monster hides in the jewelry store, etc. A mall is small and contain, but diverse enough.





After College

I was applying for job and other opportunities. There is a lot waiting. So, I decided to make a game. For both practicing programing and 3d modeling. I do not want to be rusting as I wait.

This was meant to be the first project to work on. To work and publish. As I was writing out the requirements for the gameplay style, there were too many moving parts for me at the time. There are two different gameplay styles that the player has to change between. At the time, I did not have a full grasp on architype pattern needed to achieve what I want.

The characters in the image are the founding members of a fantasy world restaurant. It is not a normal burger. It is a burger made of beef from some demon cow. The carrot cake is made from some sentient carrot. So on and so forth. From left to right their role is the following:

  • Farm hand
  • Custodian
  • Head Chef/Owner
  • Band Member/Entertainer
  • Auditor

This little guy is a robot responsible for fixing other robots. It works late hours, the graveyard shift. It struggles to move around and to do its work because it is barely 6 inches tall. It struggles more because the other robots and machines move. They should not be. The robots should be deactivated. They were turned on by themselves. A virus?

This is another attempt for a first project. Again, it was too complicated. The repair system was something; at the time, I could not figure out. It must be simple and intuitive. The puzzle needs to be absent of any language dependency. Like anagram or reading a passage that has a special code. The puzzle needs to feel natural to the world. The password is not written on the wall.

I like robots, but I do not care for the “machine takeover” story. It just feels lazy. All machines want to kill all human. Why does my toaster have that level of awareness? Who designed my toaster to argue with me? Would my toaster abandon everything that it knows to follow some strange machine’s view? What did the strange machine say to convince my toaster to kill me? These and many more questions are never answered.

That made me start thinking. Not all machines would be influenced. The virus is not effective. The words were not convincing. That specific machine was not a part of the mastermind’s plan. The machine will fulfill its purpose, no matter what.

This robot in the image is a first responder machine. Serail Name: M4Y. It a size of a SUV. It is designed for basic surveillance and aerial transportation. It was designed to be adaptable. Something was wrong when it was called in. Typically, M4Y works with a team of humans. No one else was there. But M4Y is made and was trained to perform any and all emergencies when understaffed.

The emergencies were other machines “uprising”. The machines are just causing chaos. They are making a mess, moving toward anything that made a sound, or just overheating itself to explode. M4Y tries to resolve the problem. But in the heat of the moment, would the public allow M4Y to help?

I know RPGs are not easy games to make. I know and have heard that it should not be the first game to be made. I am more interested in that genre. It is typically promoted that you have to think about your action. You cannot just go guns blazing and expect the best. RPG is not a starter project. RPGs need a large world to explore, large number of enemies, items with different uses, etc.

This project will be my first RPG. As a solo developer, I cannot do all the elements that most RPG has. The world cannot be too large, but not too small. Too Small. That made me start thinking. A small world is not the problem; it is small amount of content. I have played escape room, both in person and video games based on the concept. You can easily spend hours on it.

This game is a challenge on an RPG in one place. Games, like final fantasy, have specific strategies to win or clever options to change the outcome of fights. The fight system is about finding the right item or ability to win. But not just do massive damage to win.

At face value, it may seem that is a generic fantasy with the dragon. This is more a fantasy in the mind of a child. This is inspired by the concept of mascot horror. The consensus of mascot horror is not constant. But my understanding is as follows:

Mascot Horror is when a fictional character within the game universe is used or twisted to do a task that is not normally done by said fictional character. It is typically a cartoon character. The character can kill, do harm, or anything that is against the character’s nature. It is the idea of corrupting childhood purity. Nothing is sacred. Everyone and everything have evil.

Do I like mascot horror? It is okay. I can take it or leave it. Mainly, it does not scare me. I can get startled. It can be eerie. Maybe because I never was scared by someone in a costume before.

I was scared by a lot of things. It was mainly things I saw in the dark. See things that were not really there.

  • One time, I woke up late at night to go to bathroom. I thought I saw animal in my room. I did not have pets at the time. It was big and it was breathing. I covered my face with a blanket and waited until daybreak. It was jackets on the floor, and it was on top of a floor vent.
  • Another time, I thought a monster always stares at my house every night. It was just a streetlamp. The light was a dirty orange. It was only lamp on the whole street The lamp was covered with foliage. Branches, leaves, and vines. The wind made it sway. It looked like arms moving and teeth grinding.

Blood, gore, and violence are not needed to scare a child. Our own imagination is scaring enough. This RPG is in the mind of a child. Anything and everything can be misinterpreted as good or bad.

At this point, I have a lot of characters that were scaped. I do not mean redesign or concept art. Full on characters with background, motivation, and unique ability. They do not fit anywhere anymore. I like the design. I like the character. But they do not belong anywhere.

So, I could make a story around these characters. They are all over the place. How could I? Some were benched because they have complicated power, it does not fit the original story, or just the first on the chopping block. What type of story could I write about disposable characters as a team or at lease coexistence? These characters would never normally meet, because they were originally from different stories.

One of my first thoughts was prison. I do not want that environment. What is another place similar? We do not ask objects if they want to be moved. We do not ask a chair if it wants to be in the dinner room. A mug did not ask to be filled with coffee.

The two creatures in the image are similar to puppets. Objects of some kind. Summoned by an object like a genie from a bottle. They are stored away in some magic item vault. Some are angry or festering being locked away. The place is very old, that most forget what was archived. Somone has to find out or is a little too curious to look inside.
